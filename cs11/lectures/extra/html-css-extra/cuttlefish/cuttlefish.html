True Facts About the Cuttlefish

Cuttlefish Facts

  • Scientific name: Sepiida
  • Habitat: coastal and deep waters
  • Diet: crab, shrimp, fish
  • Predators: fish, sharks, cuttlefish
  • Length range: 15cm - 50cm
  • Weight range: 3kg - 10.5kg
  • Number of species: 120
  • Average lifespan: 1-3 years

Cuttlefish are the cutest sea creatures known to humans - baby cuttlefish are perhaps the most heart-melting sights in the sea, with tiny tentacles and adorable curvy eyes.

Despite their name, they are not actually fish. They belong to the group "cephalopods," which includes their close relatives, octopi and squid. Like squid, cuttlefish have 8 arms and two long tentacles used for feeding, and they eat prey with their "beaks". Cuttlefish, along with most cephalopods are known as the "ocean's most intelligent invertebrates."

How many sea creatures do you know of that have both a shell and fins (not flippers)? A cuttlefish is has both! The shell, or "cuttlebone" of a cuttlefish protects its organs.

Similar to the octopus, cuttlefish can use camouflage to help blend with the surroundings to hide from predators.

The "Charlie Brown" eyes of a cuttlefish serve a purpose in their survival - the W-shaped eyelids allow them to see both in front and behind them simultaneously.
